Research Participant Debrief Form for
IMPACT OF MUSIC ON ECONOMIC DECISION MAKING

Thank you for taking the time to take part in this research project.

The research project aimed to investigate:

The effects of high and low tempo music on risk aversion in relation to economic decision making.

To study the impact of music on risk aversion in context to economic decision making. -- High tempo music would illicit more risk-taking behaviour in comparison to low tempo music. The study will last for a period of around 40 days. Every participant will spend about 30-45 minutes doing the Ballon analogue risk task while being subjected to either of the three treatment groups- low tempo music, high tempo music or no music. This study is being conducted as a part of the dissertation for the MSc. Behavioural economics course.
